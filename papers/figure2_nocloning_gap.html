Figure 2. Steady-state availability — the long-run fraction of time quantum state is maintained — across three architectures with identical hardware (MTBF = 30 days, MTTR = 1 day). Classical systems achieve 99.5% via checkpoint/restore. Quantum point-to-point achieves 0.0005%: the no-cloning theorem prohibits backup, so a single hardware failure permanently destroys the quantum state. The No-Cloning Gap is 235,000×. A five-node Byzantine mesh quorum closes the gap to 99.97% by distributing rather than copying the quantum identity — no cloning required. Note the P2P bar is drawn at actual scale; its 0.002-pixel width is invisible at this resolution.
